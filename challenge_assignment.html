Plague Outbreak in India

Situation Report 1

Emanuel & Faikah

2020-02-13

1 Highlights

  • 88 suspected cases have been reported from 03 October 2019 to 25 January 2020, with 15 deaths
  • 80 of 88 suspected cases have been confirmed.
  • The case fatality rate was 9.87%
  • Household 10 had the most number of confirmed cases (8 cases).
  • There have been no new confirmed cases beyond 25 January 2020.

2 Context

Northeast India is the easternmost region of India and comprises of eight states. Its a mountaineous region covered by dense forests. It shares border with many countries and is affected by some form of conflict Hassan (2008).

3 Surveillance

The first case (case id: bjhxn) had an onset of illness on 01 October 2019. The case was reported 2 days later and diagnosed with pneumonic plague. Unfortunately, the patient had died on the same day.

Cases continued to be reported until 25 January 2020. The mean duration between reported date of onset of symptoms and reported date that of notification was 6.58 days. A total of 15 deaths has since been reported. The majority of these cases were porbable cases. The resulting case-fatality ratio is 9.87%.

A total of 88 cases were reported as suspected cases. These were reported from 03 October 2019 to 25 January 2020. The outbreak is large for a small area with a population size of 152. The peak occurance of cases was noted on 8 December 2019 with five cases. No new cases reported after 25 January 2020. There seems to be one late case on from 01 October 2019 and reported on 25 January 2020 which might be due to long incubation period.

Epidemic curve of pneumonic plague cases (n=88) in northeast India between October 2019 and January 2020, by date of onset of symptoms and status

The distribution of suspected cases among males (40) and females (48) was similar. Most notably, There were no cases for the 10-20 year age group. Most cases were reported for the 20-30 year age group with 20 and 30 cases reported by females and males, respectively. Two cases did not have information on the age of the cases.

4 Transmissibility

We modelled the transmissibility of plague in northeast Inda by fitting a log-linear regression model to the data. This analysis was conducted using the r incidence package Kamvar et al. (2019). We found that the outbreak was growing very slowly (growth rate r was 0.0102 , 95% CI: [0.0044, 0.0159]) and it took about two months (doubling time was 68.2073, 95% CI: [43.4826, 158.1102] days) for the incidence to double.
